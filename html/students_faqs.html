Frequently Asked Question


What is the eligibility criterion for students to apply for the internship and placement program?

All students who expect to graduate from the Institute by the end of the academic year 2020-2021 and are seeking employment must register for campus placements with this office by 31st of August. Placement Registration is valid for ONE ACADEMIC YEAR ONLY.Only registered students are allowed to take part in the placement process. The eligibility criteria for application may vary from company to company and may be based on CGPA or any other criteria the company feels appropriate.

What are the reputed companies which have visited this year for placements?

Around 35 companies visited our campus for placements last year. The prominent ones include: Air Asia, LnT ECC, McAfee, Deevia, Arista Networks, TCS R&I, Infosys, Aequs Aerospace, Express Analytics, BHARAT ELECTRONICS LIMITED.

How long will the placement and internship program continue for?

Placement drive and internship drive is starting from September 1, 2020 and will continue until May 2021.

Will CDC help with the preparation for placement?

The CDC, will conduct various events like mock interviews and mock coding tests in order to help prepare students for interviews.

What if there is overlapping of time between two companies during the recruitment processand the student wishes to apply to both of them?

There are a maximum of 3 companies that can conduct interviews in a day. Care will be taken so that they are hiring for different domains and their timeslots don't overlap.

How long can the placement process extend in a day?

The maximum time for any pre-assessment test (online or off-line) is 90 minutes. Under certain exceptional cases another 30 minutes may be considered. For a single company, a student can appear for a personal interview for a maximum of two hours which must include multiple sessions along with a break in between, and no single session can go beyond 45 minutes. ​ A maximum of 3 slots will be allotted per day (Internship + Placement).

Does CDC support in off-campus placements?

The CDC can’t directly support off campus placements, but information regarding offcampus placements such as eligibility, location etc will be shared with the students on a regular basis by the CDC.

Can I earn any credits through the work done in internship?

If the student wishes to get credits for the internship from the institute, the student needs topresents his/her work in the institute and submit a report (as it is for BTP) and the company must be willing to accommodate this.
This program is explicitly for the final year students and can be done in:
• 7th semester (1st July – 31st Nov)
• 8th semester (1st Jan – 30st April) *The BTP rules as mentioned in the B.Tech. rule book will be applicable
1. In-campus co-op program:
• The vacation internship can also be extended into the co-op in-campus program. • Student starts working alongside other courses ongoing in the institute.
2. On-site co-op program:
• In order to do this internship, the student must be qualified to overload his/her courses of that semester into other semesters so as to complete the minimum credits in four years
• NO courses can be completed during this semester.
• The student may spend an entire semester (along with the adjoining vacation periods if required) at the company doing an internship.

Are there any charges associated with registering for the placement or interview process?

No, there are no charges associated with the placement or interview process.

Are mechanical, civil and other core streams eligible for software jobs?

Software companies may choose to make the job offer open to only CSE students or they maybe open to students of all branches.

Can a student back out after getting selected by a company?

PSUs, Government undertakings, Ministries and Indian Armed Forces may visit the campus for placements any time. If a student secures a job at these organizations as his/her first or second offer then he/she would be de-registered from CDC.
If a student gets an offer from tier-i company, then he/she is bound to accept the offer. If a student rejects the offer, then he/she will be de-registered from CDC.
